Cheese & Chili Shorties

I've adapted this from a recipe my mother gave me. I used to love these as a child. I added the warming afterglow of chili and some seeds that I had in the house. I made this for my colleagues at work and they couldn't stop telling me how nice they were. So here's the recipe. I used a fairly ordinary supermarket-grade chili, and that seemed to work pretty well.

  • 6 oz. Plain Flour
  • 6 oz. Butter
  • 6 oz. Grated Cheddar Cheese
  • 2 Tablespoons Cream (optional)
  • 1 De-seeded & Finely Chopped Chili Pepper
  • Black Pepper to taste
  • Assorted seeds (I used a mix of sesame, onion, poppy and linseed - roughly a heaped teaspoon of each. But any small tasty seeds would probably work)
  1. Mix all the ingredients except the seeds and knead into a smooth dough.

  2. Roll out to roughly 1/4 inch thick and cut into shapes.

  3. Pour the assorted seeds onto a plate and press one side of each shape into the seeds.

  4. Place on a baking tray seedy side up. (Baking paper may help with removing them afterwards.)
  5. Bake for 15 minutes at 190 °C.

Known issues:
  • Don't overcook them - take them out of the oven when they look tasty.
  • If the dough is too hard/dry then persuading the seeds to stick may be an uphill struggle. A light egg wash on the top before pressing them into the seeds will solve this.
  • If you're short for time, or just aggressively prioritising, you can mix the seeds into the dough before baking instead of pressing them into the tops of the biscuits. They still comes out just as tasty.

Chocolate Chip Banana Cake

  • 170g Butter
  • 150g Brown Sugar
  • 275g Mashed Banana
  • 1 Egg
  • 300g Self Raising Flour
  • 100g White Chocolate Chips
  • 200g Dark Chocolate Chips
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Essence
  • 2 tsp Powdered Cinnamon
  • 2 tsp Powdered Ginger
  1. Soften the butter, if necessary.
  2. Beat in the sugar until "fluffy".
  3. Mix in the bananas, egg, vanilla essence.
  4. Mix in the flour, cinnamon, ginger and chocolate chips.
  5. Spread well onto a lined baking tray.
  6. Bake until baked (about 20mins at 180 °C

Ultimate Bacon Buttie

Well, it's my ultimate bacon buttie, YMMV. I find the sweetness of the banana and basil work really well with the bacon.

  • Bacon (obvs.)
  • Bread
  • Banana
  • Butter
  • Fresh Basil
  1. Cook the bacon as you like it.
  2. Fry the banana until soft and squishy.
  3. Toast the bread and butter it.
  4. Assemble!
