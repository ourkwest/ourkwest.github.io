Cheese & Chili Shorties

I've adapted this from a recipe my mother gave me. I used to love these as a child. I added the warming afterglow of chili and some seeds that I had in the house. I made this for my colleagues at work and they couldn't stop telling me how nice they were. So here's the recipe. I used a fairly ordinary supermarket-grade chili, and that seemed to work pretty well.

  • 6 oz. Plain Flour
  • 6 oz. Butter
  • 6 oz. Grated Cheddar Cheese
  • 2 Tablespoons Cream
  • 1 De-seeded & Finely Chopped Chili Pepper
  • Black Pepper to taste
  • Assorted seeds (I used a mix of sesame, onion, poppy and linseed - roughly a heaped teaspoon of each.)
  1. Mix all the ingredients except the seeds and knead into a smooth dough.

  2. Roll out to roughly 1/4 inch thick and cut into shapes.

  3. Pour the assorted seeds onto a plate and press one side of each shape into the seeds.

  4. Place on a baking tray seedy side up. (Baking paper may help with removing them afterwards.)
  5. Bake for 15 minutes at 190 °C.

Ultimate Bacon Buttie

Well, it's my ultimate bacon buttie, YMMV. I find the sweetness of the banana and basil work really well with the bacon.

  • Bacon (obvs.)
  • Bread
  • Banana
  • Butter
  • Fresh Basil
  1. Cook the bacon as you like it.
  2. Fry the banana until soft and squishy.
  3. Toast the bread and butter it.
  4. Assemble!
